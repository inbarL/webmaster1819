Our History
Mckinney Boyd's Computer Science Club was founded in 2015 by Thai Nguyen and Sammy Shin, who were sophomores at the time. Being incredibly talented and interested in computer science, they sought to help others learn how to program. The Engineering Club, on the other hand, has been a part of student life at Boyd since 2010, and they worked together on projects for both TSA and the Living Roof. This year, the two clubs joined forces to help everyone work together on their TSA projects, meet new people they can work with, and just see other availabale options they can try next time.

Community Service Projects
We volunteer at the Hour of Code events in schools within our community. This is an oppportunity where we provide lessons to those interested in computer science who do not have the resources to get started. We also volunteer in the Eddins Elementary Coding Club, where we teach entry and beginner lessons in programming to children from third to fifth grade, while helping to tutor kids in other TSA-related subjects, from fashion design to debate to architecture to building 3-D models.
Other Activities
Our club has joined in on many different projects to help improve our community. One of them is the Living Roof project, where the goal is to help preserve our environment. This project plans a roof that is at least partially covered with shrubs, bushes, and even small trees, all of which are planted over a waterproof surface. It can also include other layers such as a root barrier and drainage and irrigation systems to help keep the roof functioning. It would be placed above the school roof, and would provide lots of benefits to the students.
