Courses:
PRINCIPLES OF BUSINESS, MARKETING AND FINANCE
This course introduces the knowledge and skills of economics and private enterprise systems, impact of global business, marketing of goods and services, advertising and product pricing. Students analyze the sales process and financial management principles.
MONEY MATTERS
Students investigate global economics with emphasis on the free enterprise system and its impact on consumers and businesses. They will gain knowledge and skills necessary in achieving long-term financial goals through investment, tax planning, asset allocation, risk management, retirement planning and personal financial management.
BUSINESS INFORMATION MANAGEMENT I
Students implement personal and interpersonal skills to strengthen individual performance in the workplace and in society and make successful transitions to the workforce and post-secondary education. Students will apply technical skills through word processing, spreadsheet, database, and electronic presentation software.
ENTREPRENEURSHIP
This class provides students the knowledge and skills needed to become an entrepreneur. Students will learn the principles of operating a business. The primary focus of the course is to help students understand the process of analyzing a business opportunity, determining feasibility of an idea using research, and developing a plan to promote the business.
SPORTS AND ENTERTAINMENT MARKETING
Students explore a growing industry that employs athletes, musicians, advertising agents, sports agents and numerous other related professions. This course provides students with the fundamental principles and concepts identified with these industries and to develop critical-thinking and decision making skills.
MARKETING DYNAMICS
Marketing Dynamics offers knowledge and skills that help students to be proficient in one or more of the marketing functions. Students will integrate skills from academic subjects, information technology, interpersonal communication and management training to make responsible decisions. Students must secure a job for the entire school year.
